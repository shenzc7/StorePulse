Chapter 1

What is StorePulse?

StorePulse is a crystal ball for retail managers.

Imagine running a bustling supermarket. Every night, you face the same dilemma: "How many staff do I need tomorrow?" It is a delicate balancing act. If you underestimate, customers face long queues, frustration mounts, and sales are lost. If you overestimate, staff stand idle, and operational costs balloon unnecessarily.

StorePulse solves this fundamental inefficiency. It is a sophisticated desktop application that leverages Artificial Intelligence (NB-INGARCH) to predict daily footfall with high precision for the upcoming 7-14 days.

In Simple Terms StorePulse analyzes historical patterns and contextual factors—like holidays, weekends, and weather—to accurately forecast future demand.

Key Features

  • Accurate Forecasts: Generates daily footfall predictions for the next 1-2 weeks.
  • Staffing Recommendations: Translates numbers into actionable advice (e.g., "Hire 3 cashiers").
  • Inventory Alerts: Proactively warns of potential stockouts before high-traffic days.
  • Privacy First: Operates 100% offline on your local machine. No cloud dependency.
Chapter 2

Why Forecasting Fails

Most retailers rely on "gut feeling" or simplistic averages. This approach is fundamentally flawed due to the complex nature of retail data.

1. The "Average" Trap

"On average, we get 100 people on Mondays."

Reality: One Monday was a holiday (200 people). The next was rainy (50 people). The mathematical average is 125, but you rarely see exactly 125 people. Relying on the average guarantees you are wrong most of the time.

2. The "Chaos" Factor (Overdispersion)

Retail data is inherently messy. The variance—the swing between highs and lows—is often much larger than the mean. Standard statistical models like Poisson assume data is calm and predictable. Retail data is not; it is volatile.

Why NB-INGARCH is the Solution

We selected the Negative Binomial INGARCH model because it is purpose-built for this specific challenge:

  1. Count Data: It respects that customer counts are non-negative integers (you can't have 3.5 people).
  2. High Variance: It explicitly models "overdispersion," handling the chaos that breaks other models.
  3. Memory: It utilizes autoregression, remembering that a busy yesterday often implies a busy today.

Chapter 3

The Data Engine

1. Minimal Data Requirements

StorePulse is designed to be lightweight. It requires only a simple CSV file with two columns:

event_date visits
2023-01-01 145
2023-01-02 98
2023-01-03 112
Simplicity is Key Just the date and the count. The system automatically derives all other necessary features.

2. Feature Engineering (The "Brain")

From raw inputs, StorePulse constructs a rich feature set:

A. Lags (Memory)

Concept: "If 100 people came last Monday, expect around 100 this Monday."

Technical: Autoregressive Terms (Lag-1, Lag-7).

B. Calendar Context

Concept: "Is it a weekend? Is it payday? Is it a holiday?"

Technical: Exogenous Variables (Dummy variables for DOW, Holiday, Payday).

3. Data Validation (The "Bouncer")

The system enforces strict quality gates before processing:

  • Missing Values: Automatically imputed using local moving averages.
  • Negative Numbers: Flagged as errors (counts cannot be negative).
  • Zeroes: Treated as closed days or anomalies depending on context.

Step 1: Ingestion

The user drags and drops a CSV file. The system parses and validates the schema instantly.

Step 2: Enrichment

The data is cleaned, and "Brain" features (Lags, Rolling Means, Holiday flags) are generated.

Step 3: Training

The NB-INGARCH model optimizes its parameters ($\beta, \alpha, \gamma$) to fit the historical patterns using Maximum Likelihood Estimation.

Step 4: Forecasting

The model projects daily footfall for the next 14 days, providing:

  • P50 (Expected): The most likely outcome.
  • P10/P90 (Confidence Interval): The safety net (Best vs. Worst case).

Step 5: Actionable Insight

Predictions are converted into business logic:

  • "150 people expected" $\rightarrow$ "Hire 3 staff."
  • "High variance detected" $\rightarrow$ "Increase safety stock."
Chapter 5

Theory: NB-INGARCH

Viva Critical This chapter contains the core theoretical justification for the project. Read carefully.

1. Nomenclature

  • NB: Negative Binomial (The Probability Distribution).
  • IN: Integer-valued (Discrete counts: 1, 2, 3...).
  • GARCH: Generalized Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity (Modeling time-varying volatility).

2. The Case for Negative Binomial

Poisson is the standard model for count data but assumes Mean = Variance. In retail, variance is typically much larger than the mean (Overdispersion). Using Poisson leads to underestimated risk and understaffing. Negative Binomial introduces an extra parameter ($\phi$) to explicitly model this chaos.

3. The Governing Equations

Equation 1: The Conditional Mean ($\mu_t$)

Describes the expected number of customers.

$$\mu_t = \beta_0 + \sum \beta_i y_{t-i} + \sum \gamma_j x_{t,j}$$

  • $\beta_0$: Baseline traffic.
  • $\beta_i$: Influence of past traffic (Autoregression).
  • $\gamma_j$: Impact of external factors (Holidays, Weather).
Chapter 5 (Continued)

Equation 2: The Conditional Dispersion ($\phi_t$)

Describes the volatility or "risk" of the day.

$$\phi_t = \alpha_0 + \sum \alpha_i \frac{\epsilon^2_{t-i}}{\mu_{t-i}}$$

  • Captures clustering of volatility (e.g., a volatile holiday period).

4. Model Comparison

Model Suitability Limitations
Moving Average Stable, flat data Fails to react to sudden changes or trends.
ARIMA Continuous data (Stocks) Can predict negative or fractional people.
LSTM Big Data (Millions of rows) Requires massive datasets; overfits on small retail history.
NB-INGARCH Retail Counts The Optimal Choice.

Code Logic

ml/train_ingarch.py

The "Brain". Contains the fit() function which employs a Maximum Likelihood Optimizer. It iteratively adjusts parameters to maximize the probability of the observed data given the model.

api/core/forecast_service.py

The "Manager". Implements the ForecastService class, handling:

  • Caching: Retrieves recent computations to reduce latency.
  • Scenarios: Applies logic for "What-If" analysis (e.g., reducing forecast by 20% for rain).

The "Artist". Uses Recharts to render SVG visualizations of the data. It also contains the business logic for staffing rules (e.g., if visits > 100 then staff = 3).

Chapter 11

Real Retail Scenarios

Scenario 1: The Weekend Spike

Context: Saturdays typically see 2x the traffic of Mondays.

Solution: The model learns a "Day of Week" multiplier, expecting the spike and recommending doubled staffing levels.

Scenario 2: The Holiday Surprise

Context: A major holiday falls mid-week.

Solution: The is_holiday feature activates. The model applies a learned "Holiday Boost" coefficient ($\gamma$) to the forecast.

Scenario 3: The Rainy Day

Context: Forecast predicts high traffic, but heavy rain is expected.

Solution: The manager uses the "What-If" toggle. The system applies a penalty factor, lowering the forecast and reducing staff recommendations to save costs.

Chapter 12

Viva Question Bank

Top 5 Critical Questions

Q1: Why NB-INGARCH? Why not LSTM?

A: "LSTM is data-hungry, requiring millions of data points to converge. Retailers often have limited history (1-2 years). NB-INGARCH is a statistical model that performs robustly on small datasets and offers superior interpretability."

Q2: Define Overdispersion.

A: "Overdispersion occurs when the variance of a dataset exceeds its mean. In retail, traffic swings wildly (e.g., 50 vs 200). Standard models assume constant variance. Our model explicitly accounts for these swings."

Q3: How is the model validated?

A: "We utilize SMAPE (Symmetric Mean Absolute Percentage Error) for its stability with low values. We also perform a 'Rolling Origin Backtest,' simulating past forecasts to verify accuracy."

Chapter 12 (Continued)

Q4: What does Lambda ($\lambda$) represent?

A: "In this context, Lambda represents the conditional mean—the expected number of arrivals for a given time step, conditioned on past history and current features."

Q5: How are data errors handled?

A: "A validation layer intercepts data before processing. It checks for negatives, NaNs, and missing dates, imputing missing values via local moving average to prevent model failure."

Glossary

NB-INGARCH
Negative Binomial Integer-valued Generalized Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity. The core forecasting model.
Overdispersion
A statistical condition where the variance of a dataset is larger than its mean.
Exogenous Variable
External factors (Weather, Holidays) that influence the dependent variable (Visits).
SMAPE
Symmetric Mean Absolute Percentage Error. A robust accuracy metric.
Tauri
A framework for building secure, tiny, and fast binaries for all major desktop platforms.

A modern, high-performance web framework for building APIs with Python 3.7+.
